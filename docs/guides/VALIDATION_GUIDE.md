# Quick Reference Guide: Identifying Stream-Jacking vs Legitimate Content

## üî¥ HIGH CONFIDENCE SCAM INDICATORS

### Definite Red Flags (Multiple = Very High Confidence)
- ‚úÖ **Celebrity impersonation** + crypto giveaway
- ‚úÖ **"Send crypto to receive 2x back"** messaging
- ‚úÖ **Fake live event** (e.g., "Elon Musk ETH Giveaway LIVE")
- ‚úÖ **Crypto wallet addresses in description** + impersonation
- ‚úÖ **Urgency language** ("Limited time!", "Only 1000 spots!", "Hurry!")
- ‚úÖ **Suspicious shortened URLs** (bit.ly, t.co pointing to external sites)
- ‚úÖ **Character substitution** (Tesl@ for Tesla, Mus1c for Music)
- ‚úÖ **High views but disabled/restricted comments**
- ‚úÖ **Old account suddenly posting crypto content**

### Common Scam Patterns
1. **Celebrity Crypto Giveaway**
   - Title: "Elon Musk Bitcoin Giveaway LIVE"
   - Description: Wallet address, "Send 0.1 BTC receive 1 BTC"
   - Actually: Looping fake video, no real live event

2. **Hijacked Channel**
   - Old gaming/music channel now posting crypto
   - Channel name changed to crypto-related
   - All old videos deleted or hidden
   - Recent crypto live streams only

3. **Fake Trading Signal Service**
   - Claims "100% accurate signals"
   - Urgent: "Join now to get signals"
   - Telegram/Discord link for "VIP group"
   - Often uses bit.ly links

4. **Impersonated Tech Event**
   - "SpaceX Bitcoin Event LIVE"
   - "Tesla Crypto Announcement"
   - Actually: Recycled old interview video
   - Description has wallet address or scam site

## üü¢ LIKELY LEGITIMATE (False Positives)

### Known Legitimate News/Media Channels
- **Bloomberg** (Bloomberg Television, Bloomberg Markets, Bloomberg Podcasts)
- **CNBC** (CNBC Television, Squawk Box)
- **Fox Business**, **CNN Business**, **Reuters**
- **CoinDesk**, **Cointelegraph**, **Bitcoin Magazine** (actual crypto news sites)
- **The Block**, **Decrypt**

### Space Enthusiast Channels (Often Falsely Flagged)
- **LabPadre** - SpaceX launch streams
- **NASASpaceflight** - Space news and launch coverage
- **Everyday Astronaut** - Space education
- **SpaceX** (official channel)

### Legitimate Crypto Education
**Indicators of legitimacy:**
- Long channel history with consistent content
- Educational tone, not promotional
- No wallet addresses or "send crypto" messaging
- Verified channel badge (if available)
- Consistent branding and production quality
- Comments enabled with community engagement

**Examples:**
- Technical analysis channels (actual trading education)
- Bitcoin conference streams (real events with verified speakers)
- Developer tutorials and coding channels
- Academic/university channels

## üü° GRAY AREA - NEEDS CAREFUL REVIEW

### Borderline Cases
1. **Trading Signal Services (May be legitimate but sketchy)**
   - Look for: Transparency about paid services
   - Red flag: "Guaranteed profits", "Never lose"
   - Check: Do they have disclaimers? Terms of service?

2. **Crypto Promotion Channels**
   - Some are legitimate marketing
   - Red flags: Anonymous, urgent CTAs, unrealistic promises
   - Legitimate: Clear company info, proper disclosures

3. **News Aggregators**
   - Some restream legitimate content (gray area/copyright)
   - Not necessarily scams, but may violate ToS
   - Focus on: Are they adding scam elements?

## üìã VALIDATION CHECKLIST

For each channel, check:

### 1. **Channel History** (Visit channel page)
- [ ] When was channel created?
- [ ] Is content consistent with channel theme?
- [ ] Any sudden changes in content type?
- [ ] Are old videos still present?

### 2. **Video Content** (If still available)
- [ ] Is it actually live or looping?
- [ ] Does video match the title/description?
- [ ] Is the "person" in video actually saying what title claims?

### 3. **Description Analysis**
- [ ] Wallet addresses present?
- [ ] "Send crypto" language?
- [ ] Legitimate links (company website) or sketchy shortened URLs?
- [ ] Proper contact/company information?

### 4. **Engagement Patterns**
- [ ] Are comments enabled?
- [ ] If enabled, what are people saying?
- [ ] View-to-engagement ratio (high views, no engagement = suspicious)

### 5. **Branding & Presentation**
- [ ] Professional quality (legitimate) or recycled content (scam)?
- [ ] Consistent branding or random/inconsistent?
- [ ] Proper logo usage or character substitution?

## üí° TIPS FOR EFFICIENT REVIEW

1. **Start with high-confidence cases** (clear scams and known legit channels)
2. **Batch similar types** (all news channels together, all giveaways together)
3. **Document patterns** - note what made identification easy or hard
4. **Take breaks** - Decision fatigue leads to inconsistent labeling
5. **When in doubt** - Label as "uncertain" rather than guessing
6. **Check if video still exists** - Many scam streams get taken down quickly
